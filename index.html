About

Deadline for submissions: March 13th

The AFK (Away From Keyboard) game development symposium is an event designed to provide passionate SIAT undergraduate game design students with a chance to showcase their work to an industry audience. Students are encouraged to submit their game development projects to one of the four event categories listed below. From each category one project will be selected to present their work for feedback from a panel of five Vancouver games industry judges, as well as an audience of attendees. Selected students are also invited to the post-event mixer for a chance to network with Vancouver game industry officials and jumpstart their careers.

In addition to student projects the symposium will also feature two talks delivered by SIAT PhD faculty. These talks will focus on the speakers academic research involving expanding video games into areas beyond traditional entertainment. Free registration is required to attend as space is limited. To register simply visit our Eventbrite page using the 'RSVP' link located in the website header.

Panelists

Scott Inglis

Scott is an SFU Alumni from the Media Arts concentration of SIAT. He combines his design and software engineering skills to make games, both personally and professionally at East Side Games in Vancouver. As a lifelong gaming enthusiast he is excited by the new directions that are being explored every day in the industry, and he hopes to foster that kind of excitement among everyone at AFK.

Derek James

Derek James is a SIAT grad and now works as a game designer at IUGO Mobile Entertainment. Prior to joining IUGO, he worked as an independent game developer, creating and releasing mobile titles across a variety of genres.

Adam Bullied

Adam is a Game Director at Relic and works with a team of creative minds to incubate ideas for new games. (He’s pretty certain that it is the best job that a person can have.) Adam’s career in games all started when he was eleven years old, when he came across Bladerunner and Dungeons & Dragons at a local sci-fi convention. Although he didn’t understand either, he was captivated, bought his first Dungeons & Dragons set shortly thereafter and hasn’t stopped playing since. After studying film at Simon Fraser University, he taught himself early 3D software and got a job in the videogame industry at Radical Entertainment. Although it was intended to be temporary to pay off some student debt, he never managed to leave games; and at this point he doesn't think he’s qualified to do anything else

Kayla Kinnunen

Kayla is a Game Director at Roadhouse Interactive where she leads the Warhammer 40,000: Carnage team. Before arriving at Roadhouse in July 2011 Kayla spent 10 years at Electronic Arts in roles from QA tester to development director. At EA, Kayla managed many teams and projects from front-end development to audio production and has had the privilege of contributing to several leading game franchises including FIFA, NHL, NBA, Need for Speed, SSX, James Bond, Madden and Skate.

Games

Ruination

Created by: Dylan Rimmer

Ruination is a 2.5D side-scroller perspective game in which the player takes control of two characters with unique abilities and must solve various puzzles and traverse obstacles. The player will have to switch between the two characters and use their abilities of shooting and super jumping in order to solve the various puzzles and proceed through the game's narrative.

Blind

Created by: Clinton Walsh

This game is only possible because of the next-gen game engines that provide the raw horsepower needed to create this world. You travel from distant mountain tops to deep dark caves and barren deserts, all in one continuous world with no loading. And you do all this with one unique caveat… you’re blind.

Nerine

Created by: Chris Truong, Amy Truong

Nerine is a science fiction survival horror narrative that takes place in the early 2060s in an alternate universe where NASA’s Constellation Program was not cancelled. The Constellation Program is a human spaceflight program that had clearly laid out plans to send humans to populate the moon, Mars, and eventually other planets. In this reality, the lunar colony has already been well-established, and Mars has a decade old fledgling colony that has, up till the beginning of Nerine, been thriving.

Revengening

Created by: Hark Chhina, Darren Macphail, Clinton Walsh

Revenging is a top down stealth action game that requires players to successfully navigate through seven different levels and avoid detection by patrolling enemies. Being played from a top down perspective players can view the entirety of a level screen all at once including enemies, obstacles and the exit door. This is important as our game requires players to avoid each enemies’ line of sight using obstacles as cover or by attacking with punches to either kill enemies or to knock them out. An unconscious enemy can wake up over time while killed enemies are down for good.
