AFK Organization Commitee

Sean Warwick

Founder, Coordinator

Sean is a 4th year undergraduate student within the design concentration of the SIAT program. He is a designer at heart with a working understanding of numerous programming languages. This blend of skills allows him to fit easily into a variety of project roles. During his time at SIAT, Sean discovered a deep passion for game design and development. This passion led him to transition from student to teacher as a teaching assistant for the “Foundations of Game Development” course. Feeling that the SIAT program should become more involved in the Vancouver gaming industry, he created the AFK Game Development Symposium.

Andrew Chandra

Co-Founder, Event Planning

Andrew is a 4th year undergraduate student in the SIAT program at SFU. His experiences include international internships at a variety of companies including: game development, film, and cloud hosting. Recently he was employed as a SIAT research assistant. This position requires Andrew to create animation sets for a research project studying the development of responsive digital characters. Outside of school Andrew is very passionate about competitive video gaming, he joined the AFK event planning team as a way to build himself towards a future career in game design and development.

Henry Lo

Co-Founder, Event Planning

Henry is an undergraduate student within the Interactive Systems concentration of the SIAT program. He is a unique blend of a programmer and designer with a deep understanding of the UI/UX aspects of human-computer interaction. He participated in the Summer 2013 iamagamer gamejam, as well as the Microsoft hackathon in Spring 2014. He is currently employed as a research assistant in the SIAT office research lab, and as a SIAT game development ambassador for a media arts high school outreach workshop.

Scott Inglis

Co-Founder, Web Development

Scott is an SFU Alumni from the Media Arts concentration of SIAT. He combines his design and software engineering skills to make games, both personally and professionally at East Side Games in Vancouver. As a lifelong gaming enthusiast he is excited by the new directions that are being explored every day in the industry, and he hopes to foster that kind of excitement among everyone at AFK.
